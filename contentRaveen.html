What is the difference between Ayurvedic and Western Medicine

Understanding how Ayurveda differs from Western medicine is crucial for embracing its potential as a complementary approach to health. Western medicine is undeniably powerful for diagnosing diseases, treating acute conditions, and performing life-saving interventions. However, its focus on treating symptoms often overlooks the underlying imbalances causing ailments. Ayurveda takes a broader view, seeing the body, mind, and spirit as interconnected. Treatments focus on restoring balance through a combination of herbs, dietary modifications, lifestyle practices, and even spiritual guidance, addressing the root cause of disease and promoting overall wellness.
