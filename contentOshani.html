Why Choose Ayurveda
Over Western Medicine

Choosing Ayurveda over Western medicine is a decision that can vary greatly depending on
individual health needs, beliefs, and experiences. However, there are several reasons why some
people might prefer Ayurveda, especially when it comes to
holistic health and wellness.

Holistic Approach:

Ayurveda treats the body, mind, and spirit
as interconnected and seeks to balance all these aspects to achieve
health and wellness. It looks beyond symptoms to address the root causes of illness.

Natural Remedies:

Ayurveda relies on natural remedies,
including herbs, oils, and dietary changes, minimizing the risk
of side effects often associated with pharmaceutical drugs.

Long-term Wellness:

Instead of just aiming to cure or manage diseases
Ayurveda aims at achieving and maintaining long-term health and
wellness by encouraging lifestyle changes that promote overall well-being and avoiding
harmful side effects

Sustainability:

Ayurvedic practices often emphasize
sustainability and harmony with nature, using treatments that are
not only beneficial to the individual but also to the environment.

Side Effects of
Western Medicine

Side effects of Western medicine can vary widely depending on the specific
medication, the dosage, the duration of treatment, and the individuals reaction

Drug Side Effects and Reactions:

Nearly all medications have potential side effects,
ranging from mild to severe. Common side effects include nausea
dizziness, allergic reactions, and more serious effects like organ
damage or increased risk of other diseases

Dependency and Withdrawal:

Certain medications, especially those prescribed for,
pain, anxiety, and depression, can lead to physical dependence. When these medications are
discontinued, individuals may experience withdrawal symptoms,
which can be both uncomfortable and dangerous.

Antibiotic Resistance:

The overuse and misuse of antibiotics in
western medicine have led to the development ofantibiotic-resistant
bacteria. This means that infections that were once easily treatable
with antibiotics are becoming harder to cure, leading
o longer illnesses and more hospital stays.
